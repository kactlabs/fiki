# Sannakji (Korean Raw Squid with Salt)

Sannakji is a popular Korean dish consisting of raw, cut-up squid that is believed to have medicinal properties. The traditional method involves cutting the squid into small pieces and serving it in a bowl, allowing diners to eat the tentacles by hand.

---

## Ingredients

### Proteins
- 1 kg **sannakji squid**
- 2 tbsp **sesame oil**
- 1 tsp **salt**

### Sauces
- 2 tbsp **gochujang** (Korean chili paste)
- 1 tbsp **soy sauce**
- 1 tbsp **rice vinegar**

### Garnishes
- 1/4 cup **pickled radish**
- 1/4 cup **chopped scallions**
- 1/4 cup **toasted sesame seeds**

---

## Sannakji Sauce (Gochujang Sauce)

Mix the following:

- 2 tbsp **gochujang** (Korean chili paste)
- 1 tbsp **soy sauce**
- 1 tsp **rice vinegar**

---

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Prepare Squid
Cut the sannakji squid into small pieces and rinse them under cold water.

### 2. Season with Salt and Sesame Oil
Sprinkle salt over the squid pieces and drizzle with sesame oil.

### 3. Cook Squid (optional)
If you prefer your squid cooked, heat a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat and cook the squid for about 30 seconds on each side, until it's slightly firm to the touch.

### 4. Serve with Gochujang Sauce
Place the cooked squid in a bowl and pour the gochujang sauce over it. Garnish with pickled radish, chopped scallions, and toasted sesame seeds.

---

## Optional: Spicy Sannakji Variation

- For an extra spicy kick, add 1-2 tsp **gochugaru** (Korean red pepper flakes) to the gochujang sauce.
- If you prefer a creamier sauce, whisk in 1 tbsp **mayonnaise** or **korean chili paste mayonnaise**.
