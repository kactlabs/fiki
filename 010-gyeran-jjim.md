# Gyeran-jjim ([Korean Steamed Eggs])

Traditional Korean steamed eggs dish, often served as a side or used as an ingredient in various Korean recipes.

---

## Ingredients

### Proteins and Dairy
- 4 large eggs
- 1 cup milk (whole, low-fat or nonfat)

### Vegetables
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 1 small onion, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon grated ginger
- Salt and black pepper to taste

### Spices and Seasonings
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil (optional)
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce (optional)

### Additional categories as needed

---

## Gyeran-jjim Sauce (if applicable)

Mix the following:

- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon honey
- 1 teaspoon grated ginger
- 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for some heat)

---

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Prepare the Ginger and Garlic
Mince the garlic and grate the ginger, making sure to remove any excess moisture.

### 2. Sauté the Onion and Ginger
Heat the vegetable oil in a small saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the grated ginger and cook for another minute.

### 3. Whisk Together Eggs and Milk
In a large mixing bowl, whisk together eggs and milk until well combined. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.

### 4. Combine Egg Mixture with Sautéed Onion and Ginger
Add the cooked onion and ginger mixture to the egg mixture. Stir until well combined.

### 5. Steam the Eggs
Transfer the egg mixture to a heatproof bowl or a Korean-style egg steamer lined with parchment paper. Steam over boiling water for about 12-15 minutes, or until the eggs are set but still slightly moist.

### 6. Cool and Chill
Remove from heat and let cool to room temperature. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight.
