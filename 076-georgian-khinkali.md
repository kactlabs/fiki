# Georgian Khinkali (Boiled Dumplings with Spiced Meat Filling)

Georgian khinkali are traditional boiled dumplings filled with spiced meat and served as a snack or appetizer.

## Ingredients

### Category 1: Meat Filling
- 500g ground beef
- 250g ground pork
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon dried dill weed
- 1 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper

### Category 2: Dumpling Dough
- 200g all-purpose flour
- 2 eggs
- 1/2 cup water
- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

## Khinkali Sauce (Machhuli)

Mix the following:

- 100ml sour cream
- 50g chopped fresh dill weed
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- Salt and black pepper to taste

---

### Recipe Steps

#### 1. Prepare the Meat Filling
In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, pork, garlic, dried dill weed, black pepper, salt, and cayenne pepper. Mix well with your hands until just combined.

#### 2. Make the Dumpling Dough
In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, eggs, water, and vegetable oil to form a smooth dough.

#### 3. Assemble the Khinkali
Divide the dumpling dough into small balls, about the size of a small egg. Roll out each ball into a thin circle, then place a tablespoon of the meat filling in the center. Fold the dough over the filling to form a half-moon shape and press the edges together to seal.

#### 4. Boil the Khinkali
Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Carefully add the khinkali to the boiling water and cook for 10-15 minutes, or until they float to the surface.

#### 5. Serve with Machhuli Sauce
Serve the boiled khinkali hot with the machhuli sauce spooned over the top.

---

### Optional: Baked Khinkali

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the boiled khinkali on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until crispy and golden brown.
