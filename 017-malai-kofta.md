# Malai Kofta ([Indian/Pakistani Origin])

A rich and creamy Indian-inspired dish featuring marinated lamb or beef meatballs in a fragrant coconut milk sauce, served with fluffy basmati rice.

---

## Ingredients

### Meat Filling
- 500g **lamb mince** (or beef)
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 2 cloves of garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon ginger paste
- 1 teaspoon cumin powder
- 1 teaspoon coriander powder
- 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Coconut Milk Sauce
- 250ml **full-fat coconut milk**
- 150g **grated paneer** (or substitute with ricotta)
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- 1 tablespoon ghee or vegetable oil
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1/2 teaspoon coriander seeds
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Rice
- 200g **basmati rice**
- 1 cup of water
- Salt to taste

---

## Malai Kofta Sauce

Mix the following:

- 150ml **coconut milk**
- 50g **grated paneer** (or substitute with ricotta)
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- 1/2 teaspoon ghee or vegetable oil
- Salt and pepper to taste

---

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Prepare the Meat Filling
In a large mixing bowl, combine lamb mince, onion, garlic, ginger paste, cumin powder, coriander powder, garam masala, salt, and pepper. Mix well until just combined.

### 2. Shape the Koftas
Divide the meat filling into small portions (about 1 1/2 inches in diameter) and shape each portion into a ball. Flatten slightly to form a patty.

### 3. Prepare the Coconut Milk Sauce
In a medium saucepan, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and coriander seeds; let sizzle for 30 seconds. Pour in coconut milk, lemon juice, and grated paneer. Bring mixture to a simmer.
