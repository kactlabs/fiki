# Bhuna Masala ([Pakistani/Indian Description])

A rich and flavorful North Indian stew made with tender lamb or beef, simmered in a spicy sauce of aromatic spices, herbs, and vinegar.

---

## Ingredients

### Meat/Protein
- 500g boneless lamb shoulder or beef, cut into small pieces

### Aromatics
- 2 large onions, chopped
- 3 cloves of garlic, minced
- 2 inches piece of ginger, grated
- 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon powder
- 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder

### Spice Blend
- 2 tablespoons Bhuna Masala spice mix (see note)
- 1 tablespoon garam masala
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
- 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
- Salt, to taste

### Sauce
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 2 tablespoons ghee or clarified butter (optional)
- 2 tablespoons tomato puree
- 2 tablespoons vinegar (white or apple cider)
- 1 cup beef broth
- 1/4 cup water

---

## Bhuna Masala Sauce

Mix the following:

- 2 tablespoons Bhuna Masala spice mix (see note)
- 1 tablespoon ghee or clarified butter (optional)

---

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Roasting Aromatics
Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add coriander seeds and cumin seeds, let them sizzle for a few seconds, then grind into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle. Add the ground spices to the pan, stirring constantly for 1-2 minutes.

### 2. Searing Meat
Add the lamb or beef pieces to the pan, browning them on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.

### 3. Softening Onions
In the same pan, add another tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add chopped onions, stirring occasionally until they are translucent and lightly browned, about 8-10 minutes.

### 4. Adding Ginger and Garlic
Add grated ginger and minced garlic to the pan, stirring constantly for 1 minute.
