# Sambusa (Ethiopian Fried or Baked Pastry)

Sambusa is a traditional Ethiopian snack made from layers of flaky pastry filled with spiced lentils and onions.

---

## Ingredients

### Vegetable Oil
- 1 cup **vegetable oil**

### Pastry Dough
- 2 cups **all-purpose flour**
- 1/2 teaspoon **salt**
- 1/4 teaspoon **black pepper**
- 1/4 teaspoon **cayenne pepper**
- 1/4 cup **vinegar**
- 1/2 cup **warm water**

### Filling
- 1 cup **cooked red lentils**
- 1 medium **onion, chopped**
- 1 tablespoon **minced garlic**
- 1 teaspoon **ground cumin**
- 1 teaspoon **ground coriander**
- 1/2 teaspoon **ground cinnamon**
- 1/2 teaspoon **ground cardamom**
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 2 tablespoons **chopped fresh cilantro**

### Spiced Lentil Sauce (optional)
Mix the following:

- 2 tablespoons **vinegar**
- 1 tablespoon **olive oil**
- 1 teaspoon **grated ginger**
- 1 teaspoon **minced garlic**
- 1/4 teaspoon **ground cumin**
- 1/4 teaspoon **ground coriander**

---

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Prepare the Pastry Dough
Combine flour, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper in a bowl. Gradually add vinegar and warm water to form a dough. Knead for 5 minutes.

### 2. Roll Out the Pastry
Divide the dough into small balls and flatten each ball slightly. Use a rolling pin to roll out the dough to a thickness of 1/8 inch (3 mm).

### 3. Fill with Spiced Lentils and Onions
Mix cooked lentils, chopped onion, minced garlic, ground cumin, ground coriander, ground cinnamon, salt, and black pepper in a bowl. Spoon about 2 tablespoons of the filling onto one half of each dough circle.

### 4. Fold and Seal the Sambusa
Fold the other half over the filling to form a triangle or square shape. Press edges together with a fork to seal. Repeat with remaining dough and filling.
