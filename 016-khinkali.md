#  **Khinkali (Georgian Boiled Dumplings)** ([Origin/Description])

Khinkali are traditional Georgian boiled dumplings filled with spiced meat and herbs, served as an appetizer or main course.

---

## Ingredients

### Meat
- 500g **ground beef**
- 250g **ground pork**

### Aromatics
- 3 **medium onions**, finely chopped
- 2 ** cloves garlic**, minced
- 1 **teaspoon ground cumin**
- 1 **teaspoon ground coriander**
- 1/2 **teaspoon turmeric powder**
- 1/2 **teaspoon black pepper**

### Herbs and Spices
- 2 **sprigs fresh parsley**, chopped
- 1 **sprig fresh dill**, chopped
- Salt to taste

### Dough
- 250g **all-purpose flour**
- 1/4 **salt**
- 1/4 **sugar**
- 1/2 **cup warm water**

### Egg Wash
- 1 **egg**, beaten (for egg wash)

---

## Khinkali Sauce (Machhuli)

Mix the following:

- 100g **sour cream**
- 50g **diced fresh dill**
- Salt to taste

---

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Make the Dough
In a large mixing bowl, combine flour, salt, and sugar. Gradually add warm water and mix until dough forms.

### 2. Prepare Filling
In a separate mixing bowl, combine ground beef, ground pork, chopped onions, garlic, cumin, coriander, turmeric powder, black pepper, parsley, and dill. Season with salt to taste.

### 3. Assemble Dumplings
Divide dough into small balls (about the size of a golf ball). Roll out each ball into a thin circle. Place a tablespoon of filling in the center of each circle. Fold the dough over the filling to form a half-moon shape, and press edges together to seal.

### 4. Boil Dumplings
In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Carefully add khinkali and cook for 10-15 minutes, or until they float to the surface.

### 5. Serve with Sauce
Remove dumplings from boiling water with a slotted spoon and serve hot with machhuli sauce, chopped fresh parsley, and lemon
