# Shashlik (Central Asian Skewered Meat)

Shashlik is a traditional Central Asian dish of marinated meat skewers, typically made with lamb or beef, and served on a wooden spit. The cooking process involves rotating the skewers over an open flame, which results in a crispy exterior and juicy interior.

## Ingredients

### Meat
- 500g **lamb** or **beef**, cut into small cubes
- 1/4 cup **vegetable oil**
- 2 cloves **garlic**, minced
- 1 tablespoon **ground cumin**
- 1 teaspoon **ground coriander**
- 1/2 teaspoon **cayenne pepper**
- Salt and black pepper, to taste

### Marinade
- 1/4 cup **white vinegar**
- 1/4 cup **water**

### Optional: Additional Ingredients
- 1/4 cup **diced onion**
- 1/4 cup **diced bell pepper**
- 2 tablespoons **chopped fresh parsley**

## Shashlik Sauce (optional)

Mix the following:

- 2 tablespoons **mayonnaise**
- 1 tablespoon **sour cream**
- 1 teaspoon **Dijon mustard**
- Salt and black pepper, to taste

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Preparing the Marinade
Combine the vegetable oil, garlic, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper in a bowl.

### 2. Marinating the Meat
Add the lamb or beef cubes to the marinade mixture and toss to coat. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

### 3. Preparing the Shashlik Sauce (optional)
Combine the mayonnaise, sour cream, Dijon mustard, salt, and black pepper in a bowl. Refrigerate until ready to serve.

### 4. Assembling the Skewers
Thread the marinated meat cubes onto skewers, leaving a small space between each piece. If using, add diced onion and bell pepper on either side of the meat. Sprinkle with chopped parsley.

### 5. Cooking the Shashlik
Preheat a grill or outdoor cooking station to medium-high heat. Place the skewers over the heat and rotate every 2-3 minutes until the meat is cooked through and slightly charred.

### 6. Serving
Serve the shashlik hot with additional marinade sauce, if desired. Traditional
