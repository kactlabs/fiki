# Jeyuk Bokkeum (Korean Stir-Fried Pork with Vegetables)

Jeyuk bokkeum is a popular Korean stir-fry dish made with marinated pork, vegetables, and gochujang (Korean chili paste).

## Ingredients

### Meat
- 1 pound **pork shoulder or butt**, sliced into thin strips

### Vegetables
- 2 cups **mixed mushrooms** (such as shiitake and button)
- 1 cup **thinly sliced zucchini**
- 1 cup **thinly sliced carrots**
- 1/4 cup **green onions**, thinly sliced
- 1/4 cup **chopped cilantro**

### Marinade
- 2 tablespoons **soy sauce**
- 2 tablespoons **brown sugar**
- 2 tablespoons **gochujang (Korean chili paste)**
- 2 tablespoons **garlic, minced**
- 1 tablespoon **ginger, grated**

### Sauce
- 2 tablespoons **gochujang (Korean chili paste)**
- 2 tablespoons **soy sauce**
- 2 tablespoons **brown sugar**
- 2 tablespoons **rice vinegar**
- 1 tablespoon **sesame oil**

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Prepare the marinade
Mix soy sauce, brown sugar, gochujang, garlic, and ginger in a small bowl.

### 2. Marinate the pork
Place sliced pork in a large ziplock bag or a shallow dish. Pour marinade over pork and massage to coat. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes or up to 2 hours.

### 3. Prepare vegetables
Cut mushrooms, zucchini, and carrots into thin strips. Set aside.

### 4. Cook the pork
Remove pork from marinade, letting any excess liquid drip off. In a large skillet or wok, heat sesame oil over medium-high heat. Add pork and cook until browned, about 3-4 minutes per side. Transfer to a plate and set aside.

### 5. Stir-fry vegetables
In the same skillet or wok, add green onions and cook until softened, about 1 minute. Add mushrooms, zucchini, and carrots. Cook until vegetables are tender-crisp, about 3-4 minutes.
