# Shurpa (Central Asian Beef Soup)

Shurpa is a traditional Central Asian beef soup originating from Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan, and Kazakhstan. It's a hearty, comforting dish made with beef broth, beef meatballs, and various vegetables.

---

## Ingredients

### Meat
- 500g **beef mince**
- 250g **beef shank**, cut into large pieces

### Vegetables
- 2 **medium onions**, chopped
- 3 **medium carrots**, peeled and grated
- 2 **medium potatoes**, peeled and diced
- 1 **large tomato**, chopped
- 2 **fresh parsley** sprigs
- 1 **bay leaf**

### Spices and Seasonings
- 2 tsp **ground cumin**
- 1 tsp **ground coriander**
- 1 tsp **salt**
- 1/2 tsp **black pepper**
- 2 tbsp **vegetable oil**

### Broth
- 4 cups **beef broth**
- 2 cups **water**
- 2 tbsp **tomato paste**

- 2 tbsp **all-purpose flour**

---

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Prepare the Meatballs
Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the minced beef and cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until browned. Season with cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper. Let cool.

### 2. Sauté the Vegetables
In the same pan, add more oil if needed. Cook the onions, carrots, potatoes, and tomato until the vegetables are tender. Add the parsley sprigs and bay leaf; cook for an additional minute.

### 3. Make the Broth
Combine beef broth, water, and tomato paste in a large pot. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 30 minutes.

### 4. Assemble the Shurpa
Add the cooked meatballs and sautéed vegetables to the broth. Stir to combine.
