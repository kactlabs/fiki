# Plov (Central Asian Rice Dish)

Plov, also known as Pilaf or Plovah, is a traditional Central Asian rice dish made with sautéed onions, carrots, and sometimes meat, and served with raisins and fresh herbs.

## Ingredients

### Vegetables
- 2 medium-sized carrots, peeled and grated
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 2 stalks of celery, finely chopped
- 2 cloves of garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon ground coriander
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder

### Meat (optional)
- 500g lamb or beef, cut into small pieces
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

### Rice
- 2 cups long-grain rice
- Salt to taste

### Additional ingredients
- 1 cup raisins
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh dill
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice

## Plov Sauce (optional)

Mix the following:

- 1/4 cup vegetable oil
- 2 cloves of garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon ground coriander
- Salt to taste

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Prepare the ingredients
Chop the onion, carrots, and celery, and mince the garlic.

### 2. Sauté the onions and spices
Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the grated carrot, minced garlic, cumin, coriander, and turmeric powder. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.

### 3. Add meat (if using)
If using meat, add it to the pan and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks.

### 4. Add rice
Add the long-grain rice to the pan and stir well to coat with the onion mixture. Cook for about 2 minutes.

### 5. Add water and salt
Add enough water to cover the rice and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and cook until the liquid is absorbed and the rice is tender.

### 6. Fluff the Plov
Remove from heat and fluff with a fork. Add raisins, parsley, dill, and lemon juice.
