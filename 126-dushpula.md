# Dushpula (Nepali Stuffed Momo)

Dushpula, also known as Nepali Stuffed Momo, is a popular dish from the Himalayan region of Nepal. It consists of dumplings filled with spiced meat and served in a flavorful broth.

## Ingredients

### Meat
- 500g **ground beef**
- 250g **ground pork**

### Spices and Herbs
- 2 **teaspoons** ground cumin
- 1 **teaspoon** ground coriander
- 1 **teaspoon** ground cinnamon
- 1/2 **teaspoon** ground cardamom
- 1/2 **teaspoon** ground cayenne pepper
- Salt and black pepper, to taste
- 2 **tablespoons** chopped fresh cilantro
- 2 **tablespoons** chopped scallions

### Broth
- 4 cups **beef broth**
- 2 cups **water**

### Garnishes
- Chopped cilantro, for garnish
- Scallions, thinly sliced, for garnish
- Lemon wedges, for serving

---

## Dushpula Sauce (optional)

Mix the following:

- 2 tablespoons **soy sauce**
- 1 tablespoon **honey**
- 1 tablespoon **rice vinegar**

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Make the Dumplings
Combine ground beef and pork, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, cardamom, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper in a bowl. Mix well with your hands until just combined. Add chopped cilantro and scallions, and mix until just combined.

### 2. Assemble the Dumplings
Using your hands, shape the meat mixture into small balls, about 1 1/2 inches (3.8 cm) in diameter. You should end up with around 20-25 dumplings.

### 3. Cook the Broth
In a large pot, combine beef broth and water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer for at least 30 minutes.

### 4. Steam the Dumplings
Place the dumplings in a steamer basket lined with parchment paper. Place the steamer basket over the boiling broth and steam for 10-12 minutes, or until cooked through.
