# Peruvian Aji de Gallina ([Traditional Dish])

A rich and creamy chicken dish made with shredded chicken in a yellow pepper sauce, served with rice, boiled potatoes, and olives.

---

## Ingredients

### Proteins
- 1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into large pieces
- 1/4 cup white vinegar
- 1 tablespoon olive oil

### Aromatics
- 2 large onions, chopped
- 8 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 large yellow aji amarillo peppers (or substitute with 2 large yellow bell peppers)
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/2 teaspoon paprika

### Spices and Seasonings
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
- Salt to taste

### Sauce
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup grated queso fresco or feta cheese
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro

---

## Aji de Gallina Sauce (if applicable)

Mix the following:

- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 large yellow aji amarillo peppers (or substitute with 2 large yellow bell peppers), seeded and chopped
- 1/4 cup white vinegar
- Salt to taste

---

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Marinating the Chicken
In a large bowl, whisk together the vinegar, olive oil, cumin, paprika, salt, and black pepper. Add the chicken pieces and marinate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

---

### 2. Roasting the Aji Peppers
Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the aji peppers on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and roast for 30-40 minutes, or until charred and blistered. Remove from the oven and let cool.

---

### 3. Blending the Sauce
Peel the roasted aji peppers and place them in a blender or food processor. Add the garlic, white vinegar, salt, and a pinch of water. Blend until smooth.

---

Remove the chicken from the marinade, letting any excess liquid drip off. Shred the chicken into bite-sized pieces using two forks.
