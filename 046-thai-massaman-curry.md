# Thai Massaman Curry (Thai Stir-Fried Beef in Rich Curry Sauce)

Brief description of the dish: A rich and flavorful curry made with beef, potatoes, onions, and peanuts, served over steamed rice.

---

## Ingredients

### [Meat]
- 500g beef strips
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

### [Vegetables]
- 2 large onions, sliced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and cut into bite-sized pieces
- 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
- 2 cups mixed vegetables (bell peppers, carrots, green beans)

### [Spices]
- 2 tablespoons Massaman curry paste
- 2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon ground coriander
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

### [Sauce]
- 250ml coconut milk
- 100ml beef broth
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 2 tablespoons palm sugar
- 2 tablespoons tamarind paste
- 2 tablespoons chopped peanuts
- 2 kaffir lime leaves

---

## Massaman Curry Sauce

Mix the following:

- 150ml coconut milk
- 50ml beef broth
- 1 tablespoon fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon palm sugar
- 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
- 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
- 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder

---

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Marinating the Beef
In a large bowl, combine beef strips, vegetable oil, garlic, and ginger. Mix well to coat the beef evenly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes or overnight.

### 2. Cooking the Onions and Potatoes
Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add sliced onions and cook until they are lightly browned and caramelized, stirring occasionally (about 8-10 minutes). Add potatoes to the pan and cook for an additional 5 minutes.

### 3. Making the Curry Paste
In a blender or food processor, combine Massaman curry paste, cumin, coriander, turmeric powder, salt, and black pepper. Blend until you get a smooth paste.
