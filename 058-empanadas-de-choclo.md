# Empanadas de Choclo (Chilean Corn Empanadas)

Empanadas de choclo are a traditional Chilean dish, consisting of corn pastry pockets filled with a delicious mixture of ground beef, onions, and spices. They are typically served as a snack or appetizer.

## Ingredients

### Meat Filling
- 500g **ground beef**
- 1/2 cup **chopped onion**
- 2 cloves **minced garlic**
- 1 teaspoon **cumin powder**
- 1 teaspoon **paprika**
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Pastry Dough
- 2 cups **all-purpose flour**
- 1 teaspoon **salt**
- 1/4 cup **vegetable shortening**
- 1/4 cup **warm water**

### Choclo (Corn) Filling (optional)
- 1 cup **fresh corn kernels**
- 1/2 cup **heavy cream**
- 1 tablespoon **unsalted butter, melted**
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Empanadas de Choclo Sauce

Mix the following:

- 2 tablespoons **vegetable oil**
- 2 cloves **minced garlic**
- 1 teaspoon **cumin powder**
- 1/4 cup **chopped fresh cilantro**

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Prepare Meat Filling
In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, cumin powder, and paprika. Cook until the onion is translucent.

### 2. Prepare Pastry Dough
Combine the flour and salt in a bowl. Add the vegetable shortening and, using a pastry blender or your fingertips, work it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the warm water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball.

### 3. Roll Out Pastry Dough
Divide the pastry dough into four equal pieces. Roll out each piece into a thin circle, about 1/8 inch thick.

### 4. Fill Empanadas
Place a tablespoon of meat filling in the center of each pastry circle. If using choclo filling, spoon it on top of the meat filling. Fold the pastry dough over to form a half-moon shape, pressing the edges together with a fork to seal.
