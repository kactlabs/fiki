# Dhima Pitha (Bengali Sweet Rice Cake)

Dhima pitha is a traditional Bengali sweet rice cake made with glutinous rice flour, sugar, and ghee. It's often served at special occasions like weddings and festivals.

---

## Ingredients

### Dry Ingredients
- 1 cup **glutinous rice flour**
- 1/2 cup **all-purpose flour**
- 1 teaspoon **salt**

### Wet Ingredients
- 1 cup **sweetened condensed milk**
- 1/4 cup **ghee, melted**
- 2 large **eggs**
- 1 tablespoon **rosewater (optional)**
- 1/4 teaspoon **cardamom powder**

### Additional Ingredients
- 1 cup **chopped pistachios or almonds for garnish (optional)**

---

## Dhima Pitha Sauce (optional)

Mix the following:

- 2 tablespoons **sweetened condensed milk**
- 1 tablespoon **ghee, melted**

---

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Preparing the Dough
Combine glutinous rice flour, all-purpose flour, and salt in a large mixing bowl.

### 2. Adding Wet Ingredients
Add sweetened condensed milk, melted ghee, eggs, rosewater (if using), and cardamom powder to the dry ingredients. Mix well until a smooth dough forms.

### 3. Kneading the Dough
Knead the dough for about 10 minutes, until it becomes soft and pliable.

### 4. Resting the Dough
Rest the dough for at least 30 minutes.

### 5. Shaping the Cake
Divide the dough into small portions, depending on how large you want your dhima pitha to be. Shape each portion into a round cake.

### 6. Steaming the Cakes
Steam the cakes over boiling water for about 10-15 minutes, or until they're cooked through and slightly firm to the touch.

### 7. Cooling and Serving
Let the cakes cool before serving. You can garnish them with chopped nuts if desired.

---

## Optional: Dhima Pitha Variations

To make a chocolate version of dhima pitha, add 1-2 tablespoons of cocoa powder to the dry ingredients. To make a fruit-filled version, fill the steamed cakes with your favorite fruits (such as strawberries or blueberries) before serving.
