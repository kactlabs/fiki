# Beef Rendang Iambu Jack (Malaysian Minangkabau-Inspired Stew) ([Sarawakian Beef Stew])

A rich, spicy beef stew originating from the Minangkabau people of Sarawak, Malaysia, flavored with coconut milk and a blend of spices.

---

## Ingredients

### Meat
- 500g **beef brisket or chuck**, cut into 2-inch pieces

### Spices
- 4 **cinnamon sticks**
- 3 **clove buds**
- 1 **star anise pod**
- 2 **long green chilies**, sliced
- 1 **teaspoon** **ground cumin**
- 1 **teaspoon** **ground coriander**

### Aromatics
- 4 **kaffir lime leaves**
- 2 **fresh lemongrass stalks**, bruised
- 2 **shallots**, sliced

### Coconut Milk and Sauce
- 250ml **coconut milk**
- 100g **palm sugar**
- 2 **tablespoons** **tamarind paste**

---

## Rendang Sauce

Mix the following:

- 150g **palm sugar**
- 50g **coriander seeds**
- 1/4 teaspoon **cayenne pepper**
- 2 tablespoons **coconut oil**

---

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Roasting and Grind
Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C). Place cinnamon sticks, clove buds, star anise pod, and coriander seeds on a baking sheet and roast for 10 minutes. Let cool, then grind into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.

### 2. Marinating
In a large bowl, combine beef pieces, palm sugar, ground cumin, salt, and black pepper. Mix well to coat the beef evenly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

### 3. Saute Aromatics
Heat 2 tablespoons of coconut oil in a large Dutch oven over medium heat. Add kaffir lime leaves, lemongrass stalks, and shallots. Cook until fragrant and the shallots are translucent.

### 4. Adding Spices and Beef
Add ground spice mixture (roasted spices + cayenne pepper) to the pot and stir well. Add beef pieces and cook until browned
