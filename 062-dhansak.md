# Dhansak (Indian Stir-Fried Lentils and Meat)

Dhansak is a popular Indian dish made with lentils, meat, and a variety of spices. It's often served in the state of Gujarat, where it's a staple food.

---

## Ingredients

### Proteins
- 500g boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 250g lamb or beef, cut into small pieces

### Spices and Aromatics
- 2 medium onions, chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 medium tomatoes, diced
- 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
- 2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon ground coriander
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
- 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
- Salt, to taste

### Lentils and Grains
- 250g split red lentils (masoor dal)
- 2 tablespoons brown rice flour
- 2 tablespoons semolina (rava)

### Oils and Ghee
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter), for serving

---

## Dhansak Sauce

Mix the following:

- 2 cups water
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 2 tablespoons tomato puree
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon ground coriander
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
- Salt, to taste

---

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Prepare the Spice Paste
Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add onions and sauté until they're lightly browned. Add garlic, ginger, cumin, coriander, turmeric, and red chili powder. Cook for another minute.

### 2. Cook the Lentils
In a separate pot, cook the lentils with 2 cups of water over medium heat until they're tender. Drain and set aside.

### 3. Sear the Meat
Heat oil in a pan over high heat. Add the meat pieces and sear them until they're browned on all sides. Remove the meat from the pan and set it aside.
