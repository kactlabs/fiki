# Ethiopian Red Lentil Stew (Misir Wot) ([Ethiopian Descent])

A hearty, comforting stew originating from Ethiopia, made with red lentils and a blend of aromatic spices.

---

## Ingredients

### Proteins
- 1 cup **dried red lentils**
- 2 cups **chicken or vegetable broth**

### Aromatics
- 2 medium **onions**, chopped
- 3 cloves **garlic**, minced
- 2 medium **carrots**, peeled and chopped
- 2 stalks **celery**, chopped

### Spices
- 1 tsp **ground cumin**
- 1 tsp **ground coriander**
- 1/2 tsp **ground turmeric**
- 1/2 tsp **ground cinnamon**
- 1/4 tsp **cayenne pepper**

### Other
- 2 tbsp **vegetable oil**
- Salt and black pepper, to taste
- 2 tbsp **niter kibbeh** (Ethiopian clarified butter) or substitute with regular butter

---

## Misir Wot Sauce

Mix the following:

- 1/4 cup **red wine vinegar**
- 2 tbsp **honey**
- 1 tsp **ground cinnamon**
- 1/4 tsp **cayenne pepper**

---

## Recipe Steps

### 1. Preparing the Lentils
Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onions, garlic, carrots, and celery; sauté until the vegetables are tender.

---

### 2. Adding Spices and Broth
Add cumin, coriander, turmeric, cinnamon, and cayenne pepper; stir for 1 minute. Pour in broth and bring to a boil.

---

### 3. Simmering Lentils
Reduce heat to low and add lentils. Cover the pot and simmer for 20-25 minutes or until the lentils are tender.

---

### 4. Preparing the Sauce
In a small bowl, whisk together vinegar, honey, cinnamon, and cayenne pepper until smooth.

---

### 5. Serving
Stir the sauce into the stew. Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Serve hot, garnished with niter kibbeh or regular butter if desired.

---
